Emergency First Response First Aid (Pediatric)

This 1 day course breaks down into two parts, these being First Response and Secondary care.

Child first aid is more critical to get right, a child will deteriorate far faster than an adult and so care must be given quickly, this course teaches you how to care for an ill or injured child or infant and follows much the same lines as our emergency first response course.

In this course you will learn that it’s not just the child you need to treat but the parents/guardian as well, panic cause parents/guardians to resist your help and you need to break down that barrier quickly to help prolong the lifeline.

In First Response you will learn to deal with a none breathing casualty, learning how to recognise signs and how to administer rescue breathing and Cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR), you will also learn how to deal with serious bleeding, spinal injury and shock as well as scene assessments and barrier use and other life threatening issues.

In Secondary care you will learn how to look after a patient with pulse and breathing whilst waiting for emergency help, learn how to place a patient into the three point prone (recovery position) how to do a head to toe check to look for signs of injury, how to write a patient check list to help the emergency services when they arrive as well as bandaging and splinting, learn how to deal with choking, how to deal with heat related problems and other medical conditions.
